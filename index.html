Developement of the Macintosh Computer Display Size

  • The First Mac


    First Apple Macintosh Computer, 1984. Credit: Apple
    Apple releases the first Macintosh Computer in 1984 that featured a 9-inch monochromatic 512 x 342 pixel display. The Macintosh was the first successful mass-market all-in-one desktop personal computer with a graphical user interface, built-in screen, and mouse. It played a pivotal role in establishing desktop publishing as a general office function. The motherboard, a 9 in (23 cm) CRT monitor, and a floppy drive were housed in a beige case with integrated carrying handle; it came with a keyboard and single-button mouse. It sold for US$2,495. Sales of the Macintosh were strong at its initial release on January 24, 1984, and reached 70,000 units on May 3, 1984. Upon the release of its successor, the Macintosh 512K, it was rebranded as the Macintosh 128K. The computer's model number was M0001.

  • The Macintosh II


    Image: Alexander Schaelss, Wikipedia
    3 years later, Apple released the Macintosh II, which had a larger 12 to 13 inch display. Based on the Motorola 68020 32-bit CPU, it is the first Macintosh supporting color graphics. When introduced, a basic system with monitor and 20 MB hard drive cost US$5,498. The Macintosh II was the first computer in the Macintosh line without a built-in display; a monitor rested on top of the case like the IBM Personal Computer and Amiga 1000. It was designed by hardware engineers Michael Dhuey (computer) and Brian Berkeley (monitor) and industrial designer Hartmut Esslinger (case).

  • The iMac G3


    Image: Alterations by David Fuchs; original by Rama, licensed CC-by-SA - This file was derived from: IMac-IMG 7043.jpg
    Apple next released the iMac G3 in 1998. The display size on this release was 15 inches. The iMac's all-in-one design is based around a cathode-ray tube display; the G3 processor, components, and connectivity were all included in a single enclosure. Apple's head of design Jony Ive and his team developed a teardrop-shaped, translucent plastic case that was a radical departure from the look of the company's previous computers.

  • The iMac G4


    Image: Photo taken by uploader, all rights released, Wikipedia
    Apple released the iMac G4 in 2002 with a display size of 17 inches. The internals such as the hard drive and motherboard were placed in the "dome" instead of the LCD panel as it would be too heavy. The arm allowed the display to hold almost any angle around the dome-shaped bottom. During this time, Apple had all but eliminated CRT displays from its product line. However, the LCD iMacs were unable to match the low price point of the iMac G3, largely due to the higher cost of the LCD technology at the time. The internal components are housed in a 10.6" half-sphere, ice white case with a chromed stainless steel neck that supports a 15" TFT active matrix LCD display.
